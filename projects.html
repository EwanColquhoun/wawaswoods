Recent Projects

Dolls Cots

This was a commission for a local nursery. It is made from exterior grade plywood. The requirement was for two, child friendly, dolls cots. I made them able to rock gently to help get those dolls to sleep. It was finished with pink and purple paint at the client's request.
The main construction processes were:

- Cut materials, in duplicate.
- Glue and fix structure together.
- Sand to remove any rough surfaces.
- Apply primer coats and once dry apply final colour (numerous coats) and sealer.

Height Chart

Made from ash, this lovely piece shows the wonderful stages of a child's growth. Made for a first birthday present it now proudly hangs on the wall. Complete with wooden peg and personalised name pyrography. Subsequently we have done a similar project but for twins. It had two vertical rows of holes representing the height of each twin. As it happens there is a matching one under construction at the moment for Ava's sibling.

The main construction processes were:
- Prepare live edge board (remove bark, sand, cut to size).
- Measure and mark out routered sections.
- Use the router and drill to make all the neccessary marks on the board.
- Sand and apply the various finish coats (Osmo oil in this case).

Walker

This was a fun project to make. We wanted to make it both functional and exciting for a child's mind. The client gave me the freedom of design here to accomplish what she wanted. The body is made from larch, the rungs are oak and it has coloured epoxy-resin wheels. The wheels are coated with rubber so as to maintain grip on most surfaces. It has 3 levels of handles so it can grow with the client's children.
The main construction processes were:

- Machine the larch to the require measurements.
- Cut all joints and fit the piece together.
- Sand the body section.
- Make and prepare the epoxy items.
- Apply finish to the body section. Once dry, assemble the walker.
